Mission Statement

The MTHS Computer Club strives to find a community of computer-savvy and technologically advanced individuals while retaining an open forum for new, unexperienced people. Each individual strand embodies a separate purpose, channeling separate motives under one, singular identity. Within the computer club, strands specialize in specfic motives, including the creation and regulation of software products for the school, hardware production and dissection, computer event participation, gaming and more! The Computer Club includes the Information Technology (IT), Hardware, Software, and Entertainment strands respectively (as shown below).

Strand Motive

The purpose of the Information Technology strand of the Computer Club is to create innovate software programs and potentially put them into use within the MTHS club and school atmosphere, regulate and gradually complexify the MTHS clubs website (MTHS ClubWARE), and work collaboratively with the Software Strand on training new members. Our talented team of executives retain programming and software experience, and our goal is to provide those facilities to new members. Not only do we offer our members the oppurtunity to create software collaboratively as a team, but we applicate our technology into real use within the school. Members have the oppertunity to work in teams and create software, diversifying their portfolios for professionial development in the future.

Group Development

The IT strand prides itself in allowing its members to freely express their opinion on technology and seek innovation with computer software. We allow our members to find problems in their personal environment and collaboratively create solutions to those problems with technology. By working together, members work portions of a main solution. At the end of each project, members recieve merit for their collaberation and hard work in the form of a project that can be presented for important steps towards a career in the future.

Strand Motive

The Hardware Strand of the Computer Club enables its members to learn about and work with a diverse range of electronic hardware. With unique projects that work on both vintage and new hardware, members of the Hardware Strand can take part in a truly unique and unforgettable experience where anyone can utilize their current knowledge about hardware and build on tech based skills like the ability to repair, which can be crucial in a modern world that revolves around technology.

Strand Expectations

The Hardware Division of the Computer Club contains exciting and educational activities that allow its members to build on existing skills and gain new ones. Expect to take part in the building and disassembly of computers, as well as diagnosing and repairing various electronic devices. In addition, the Hardware Division will take an active role in reducing electronic waste by working to acquire hardware that others deem obsolete and refreshing them.

Strand Motive

The purpose of the Software Strand is to work together in creating new and innovative computer programs, to teach new members to program, and to organize and compete in competitions, such as the ACSL (American Computer Science League). We have many talented computer programmers that currently lead these projects and are always ready to include those without any experience at all. The best way to learn programming is through experience, so we provide the opportunity to have that experience. Our members will work through these projects, with the goal being that they will eventually be leading these projects themselves and competing in competitive events such as ACSL.

New Member Policy

Whether you have never touched a computer before or you are the best programmer to have set foot in the school, the Software Strand is always looking for new members. Those without any experience will be trained by our Secretary of Education, whose whole job is to make sure that new members are able to get a solid foundation in computer programming, to then be able to try out their new skills on real projects. The experienced members will be able to jump right into these projects and even ACSL if they join before the year's deadline. Probably the most valuable thing about this strand is that members will be working on real and useful projects. This will prepare members for the real world, where large projects are too big to be done by one person alone. Every member will be able to contribute and see their idea come to life.

Strand Motive

The main purpose of the Entertainment Strand of the Computer Club is to allow students to use computer after school for their own means. We encourage people to try to learn new things using the computers provided by the school. The main project for the end of the school year is for the entertainment division of the computer club to program a video game. Prior coding knowledge is not necessary, as we will guide and teach members how to code.

Growing Community

We are allowing more and more students to join our club and use our school's computers for themselves, whether it be for personal entertainment or homework. However, we just ask that members contribute their time to participating in the activities and fundraising events. The board members try their best to accommodate the requests of the members of the club. Occasionally, there will be surveys released to determine what we should do for upcoming events. Therefore, we ask that everyone contribute and fill out these forms accordingly.
