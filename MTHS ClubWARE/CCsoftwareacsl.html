What Is It?

The American Computer Science League consists of 4 tests that are taken throughout the year, each having three different topics to be tested. Each test contains a 30 minute in-school short answer test as well as an out-of-school 72 hour programming problem. Currently, we are competing in Intermediate-5 where the top 5 scores will be sent to see whether we qualified for All-Stars, the national event for the best schools. This makes the ACSL a team event, meaning that the most experienced club members must work together to prepare for these tests. We are always looking for new talent to compete in this event, so make sure to check out the topics list below. Please know it is not necessary to be familiar with all the topics; most of us will need to learn a decent amount of them for the first time. A solid foundation in computer science will be sufficient in doing well.
